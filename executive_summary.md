# Executive Summary

## Objective

This analysis evaluates the impact of a newly launched **Team Collaboration Feature** on user behavior and business outcomes for a SaaS collaboration platform.

The product team requested an analysis to determine whether the feature improves:

- Feature adoption
- User retention
- Subscription revenue

The goal is to inform the decision of whether the company should expand investment in the feature.

---

## Key Questions

The analysis addresses the following questions:

1. What percentage of users adopt the feature?
2. How quickly do users adopt it after signup?
3. Do feature adopters retain better than non-adopters?
4. Does feature adoption increase subscription revenue?
5. Are certain user segments more likely to adopt the feature?

---

## Key Findings

### Strong Feature Adoption

Approximately **87% of eligible users adopt the collaboration feature**, indicating strong interest among users who are exposed to it.

Adoption rates increase significantly with baseline engagement.

Users with higher activity in their first week are more likely to adopt the feature.

---

### Significant Retention Improvement

Feature adopters retain significantly better than non-adopters.

Across the first four weeks after signup:

- Adopters retain **10–30 percentage points higher** than non-adopters.
- The retention advantage persists even after controlling for baseline engagement levels.

This suggests the feature meaningfully improves product stickiness.

---

### No Immediate Revenue Increase

Revenue analysis before and after feature adoption shows **no significant short-term increase in monthly revenue**.

This indicates the feature primarily drives:

- engagement
- retention
- product value

rather than immediate monetization.

---

## Product Recommendation

Based on the analysis:

**Expand the feature rollout and improve onboarding for early adoption.**

Rationale:

- Strong adoption among eligible users
- Significant retention improvements
- Increased long-term customer lifetime value potential

Recommended next steps:

- Increase feature visibility during onboarding
- Expand feature availability to a larger portion of the user base
- Monitor long-term revenue expansion among adopters
